Platform Analysis

Shopify: right for Phase 1,
not the long-term answer

Shopify provides the fastest path to a functional D2C storefront. But it has architectural ceilings that matter as KeralaKart scales toward a marketplace model.

✓ Phase 1 — Shopify strengths
✅3–4 week time-to-market with zero backend engineering
✅Native QBO integration via Synder — automated reconciliation
✅Mature Klaviyo + WATI integrations, production-proven
✅Shop Pay — one-tap checkout with saved addresses
✅Stripe-backed payment processing with Shopify Payments
✅Built-in inventory tracking, fulfilment, and reporting
✕ Shopify limitations at scale
❌2.4–2.9% transaction fee compounds at high GMV
❌No native multi-vendor marketplace architecture
❌Vajro = WebView shell, not native app performance
❌API rate limits restrict high-frequency integrations
❌Advanced reporting locked behind Shopify Plus tier
❌No path for custom logistics / driver dispatch layer

💡
Direct answer — Is Shopify expensive long-term?
Yes — but only past a specific GMV threshold, and that's exactly when Phase 3 is planned.
PHASE 1 CEILING
$50K/mo GMV
Start headless migration. Transaction fees becoming a meaningful line item.
HARD EXIT POINT
$300K/mo GMV
~$9,100/mo ($109K/yr) in Shopify costs alone. Custom platform ROI is under 12 months.
PHASE 3 SAVES
$87K+/yr
Eliminated Shopify fees at $300K GMV/mo. Funds Phase 3 infra within one year.
The architecture accounts for this explicitly. Phase 1 locks in Shopify's fast launch. Phase 2 decouples the frontend (headless) — so when transaction fees cross the pain threshold, the switch to the custom backend (Phase 3) is surgical, not a full rebuild. The 36-month plan is designed so Shopify's cost curve never catches KeralaKart off guard.
Shopify Plan Selection

Picking the right Shopify plan

Start on Basic. The lower transaction fee on the Shopify plan justifies the upgrade at 50+ orders/day — the math works out clearly.

Plan Monthly Staff Accounts Transaction Fee Reports Recommended for
Basic $79 CAD 2 2.9% Basic 0–50 orders/day · Launch phase
Shopify $105 CAD 5 2.4% Professional 50–200 orders/day · Growth phase
Advanced $399 CAD 15 2.1% Advanced + custom 200+ orders/day · Pre-Phase 2
Plus $2,300+ CAD Unlimited 0.15–0.25% Full analytics Only if staying on Shopify at scale
Start on Basic. Upgrade to Shopify plan when D2C hits 50+ orders/day — the lower transaction fee (2.4% vs 2.9%) alone justifies the upgrade at that volume. Do not jump to Advanced or Plus until it is clearly necessary.
Core Integrations

Launch-critical integrations

These five integrations must be configured before soft launch. Each is directly tied to a critical business operation — accounting, fulfilment, customer communication.

📊
Synder → QuickBooks Online
Syncs every Shopify order as a QBO invoice. Maps D2C Sales, Delivery Revenue, and Payment Processing Fees to the correct QBO accounts. Runs daily at 2am. Configure and run a $1 test order before launch.
Phase 1
📧
Klaviyo
Triggered automated flows: welcome series (3 emails), abandoned cart (1hr + 24hr), order confirmation, delivery notification, post-delivery review request (3 days), win-back (60 days inactive), restock alert.
Phase 1
💬
WATI / Interakt — WhatsApp Business API
Order confirmations and delivery notifications via WhatsApp in parallel with email. Broadcast list for new arrivals, weekend specials, restock alerts. WhatsApp chat widget on site (bottom-right). Migration broadcast with KARAVAN10 discount.
Phase 1
📱
Vajro — Mobile App Wrapper
Converts Shopify store to branded iOS + Android app. Push notifications (5× email open rate), App Store + Play Store submission handled by Vajro dashboard. Apple Developer account ($130/yr) + Google Play account ($30 one-time) required. Submit end of Month 2.
Phase 1
📦
Inventory Management
Launch: Shopify native inventory tracking. Phase 2: Stocky by Shopify (free) or Cin7 Core. Phase 3 (xkart.ca): Cin7 Core or Brightpearl for multi-location, multi-vendor inventory. Set reorder alerts at 3-week supply for Tier 1 SKUs, 10% safety buffer on counts.
Phase 2 upgrade
🔍
Algolia Search
Replaces Shopify's native search in Phase 2. Millisecond results with faceted filters, Malayalam + English support, typo tolerance, autocomplete. Syncs product catalogue via Shopify webhooks. Required before native app launch.

Product Catalogue

Category structure & SKU tiers

Collections are structured around how a Kerala household actually shops — not generic product type taxonomy. Tier classification drives inventory strategy.

Rice & Grains
Tier 1 · Karavan
Core staple — always in stock. Track inventory, no backorder. Reorder at 3-week supply threshold.
Matta rice · Red rice · Puttu podi · Idiyappam flour · Rava
Coconut Products
Tier 1 · Karavan
High-frequency purchase. Featured on homepage. Never allow out-of-stock on Tier 1.
Coconut milk · Coconut oil · Desiccated coconut
Spices & Masalas
Tier 1 · Karavan
Differentiator SKUs — authentic Kerala sourcing. Key SEO category for Canada search traffic.
Kerala black pepper · Cardamom · Kerala garam masala
Pickles & Chutneys
Tier 1 · Karavan
High margin, long shelf life. Karavan brand differentiation strongest here.
Mango pickle · Lime pickle · Fish pickle
Lentils & Pulses
Tier 2 · Weekly JIT
Ordered weekly from GTA distributor. Thursday order → Monday fulfilment cycle. Allow backorder.
Toor dal · Urad dal · Chana dal · Moong dal
Fresh & Frozen
Tier 2 · Weekly JIT
Cold-chain required. Delivery coordination with partner must happen same-day. Note in checkout.
Frozen Kerala fish · Frozen vegetables · Banana leaves
Snacks & Namkeen
Tier 1 · Karavan
Taxable category (HST applies). Configure Shopify tax codes correctly — snacks are NOT HST-exempt.
Banana chips · Tapioca chips · Murukku · Chakli
Beverages
Tier 3 · On-demand
Purchased after order placed. Enable "continue selling when out of stock". Display delivery lead time in product description.
Malabar tea · Filter coffee powder · Rose milk mix
