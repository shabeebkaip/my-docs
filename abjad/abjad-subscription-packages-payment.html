Teacher Subscription — Ranking Algorithm

1.1 Problem Statement

When multiple teachers subscribe to gain top visibility, a simple "pay first, rank first" approach creates unfairness, stale listings, and poor experience for schools. The platform requires a smart, multi-factor ranking system that rewards profile quality, genuine activity, and verified credentials — not just payment order.

Key Challenge: Without a dynamic ranking mechanism, early subscribers permanently monopolise top positions, discouraging new teacher sign-ups and reducing result quality for schools.

1.2 Recommended Approach: Weighted Dynamic Ranking Score (WDRS)

Subscribed teachers are placed in a Premium Pool and ranked among themselves using the Weighted Dynamic Ranking Score (WDRS). Non-subscribed teachers are ranked separately below the premium pool.

Best Option: Hybrid Quality + Subscription Ranking — combines subscription investment with genuine teacher quality signals, ensuring schools always see the most relevant candidates first.
WDRS = (Profile Score × 30%) + (Subscription Tier × 20%) + (Activity Score × 20%) + (Response Rate × 15%) + (Verification Score × 15%)
Maximum possible score: 100 points

Scoring Breakdown:

Factor Weight How It Is Calculated Max Points
Curriculum Experience 35% Number of curriculum types taught (e.g. MoE, IGCSE, IB, American) + years of experience — higher count ranks first 35
Qualifications Count 35% Total number of verified degrees, certificates, and specializations — the more qualifications, the higher the rank 35
Subscription Tier 20% Annual = 20 pts  |  6-Month = 14 pts  |  Monthly = 8 pts  |  Free = 0 pts 20
Activity Score 10% Last login recency + response rate to interview invitations 10
Tie-Breaking Rule: When two teachers have an equal WDRS, the one with the earlier subscription start date ranks higher.
Anti-Monopoly Rotation: Subscribed teachers in the top positions rotate every 24 hours among those within equal score bands (±5 points of each other) to ensure fair exposure and prevent permanent position lock-in.

  • Admin can adjust WDRS weights from the Admin Panel (Feature 4.7.4) without code changes
  • Admin can manually review if a subscribed teacher is gaming the system or inflating activity metrics
  • Suspicious activity flag removes premium visibility boost pending administrative review

Single school subscription plan available in three billing durations:

Plan Billing Period Total Price (excl. VAT) Effective Rate

School Plan Annual 13,000 SAR / year ~1,083 SAR/mo
VAT (15%) applies to all prices. Prices above are exclusive of VAT. Longer billing periods offer a reduced effective monthly rate.
Recurring Payments: School subscriptions renew automatically. Schools may opt out at any time — cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period with no refunds for unused time.
Card Storage: Card details are stored securely via the payment gateway for automatic recurring billing.
Bank Account Payments: Clients may also pay via local bank transfer. Abjad bank account details (local KSA bank) will be provided by the client upon request.
Zakat (CR Number): Zakat obligations are handled manually by the client through their CR number to avoid additional monthly platform charges. Abjad does not process Zakat on behalf of subscribing schools.
Admin Panel Language: The school admin panel must be available in both Arabic and English. The Arabic version will be sent to the client for review before launch; an English version will also be provided.

2.1.5 School Trial Period

Free 5-Day Trial — Schools can sign up for free and access a limited version of the platform for 5 days before committing to a paid plan.
  • Only 3 teacher CVs visible during the trial period
  • Limited access to candidates — full candidate search and contact features are locked
  • Admin panel sections inaccessible — full admin panel unlocks only after subscribing to a paid plan
  • No card required to start the trial — school signs up with basic details

Payment Flow & ZATCA Compliance

3.1 What is ZATCA E-Invoicing?

The Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) — هيئة الزكاة والضريبة والجمارك — mandates e-invoicing (Fatoorah / فاتورة) for all VAT-registered businesses in Saudi Arabia.

Phase 1 — Generation
Generate and store e-invoices in ZATCA-approved XML format with cryptographic QR codes for all transactions.
Phase 2 — Integration
Real-time invoice reporting to ZATCA via API using Cryptographic Stamp Identifier (CSID) for B2B transactions.
VAT Rate
15% on all taxable transactions — hardcoded to avoid calculation errors.
Invoice Archiving
All invoices must be stored and accessible for a minimum of 6 years from invoice date — non-negotiable.

3.2 Invoice Types Required

Transaction Type Customer Type Invoice Type Requirements
Subscription Payment School (B2B) Standard Tax Invoice Buyer VAT number, XML format, QR code, UUID
Teacher Subscription Teacher (B2C) Simplified Tax Invoice Seller details, QR code, UUID — no buyer VAT required

3.3 Payment Provider — Under Review

Payment provider is currently under discussion and has not been finalised. The options below are being evaluated. The document will be updated once a decision is confirmed.

Option A — Salla / Zid as Payment Storefront (Under Investigation)

The team is exploring whether Salla (salla.com) or Zid (zid.sa) — both Saudi-based e-commerce platforms — can be used as the payment layer. The key advantage is that both platforms are already registered and licensed in Saudi Arabia, which could remove the need for Abjad to independently register as a payment entity in KSA.

The proposed approach would be:

  1. Create the Abjad subscription packages (Teacher Premium — 3 durations, School Plan — 3 durations) as products on Salla or Zid
  2. User is redirected to the Salla/Zid checkout to complete payment
  3. On successful payment, the platform sends a webhook notification back to the Abjad system to activate the subscription
  4. User is redirected back to the Abjad platform with their subscription active
Feasibility findings (preliminary):
  • Salla — supports webhooks for order.created, subscription.charge.succeeded, and payment events. Has a Recurring Payments API. Requires a merchant account with a KSA commercial registration or freelance certificate.
  • Zid — supports webhooks for order creation and status updates. Supports subscription app model. Also requires KSA-based registration for merchants.
  • Post-payment redirect — partial support on Salla; not clearly documented for Zid. Further confirmation needed directly from both platforms.
  • Key open question: Whether either platform allows a third-party SaaS (Abjad) to use them purely as a payment layer, with the checkout hosted on Salla/Zid but the subscription managed entirely in the Abjad system.
Risk: Both Salla and Zid are e-commerce storefronts, not payment gateway APIs. Merchant registration still requires a legal entity in Saudi Arabia (commercial registration or freelance certificate). If Abjad does not have a registered KSA entity, this option may not be directly usable — however, a local partner arrangement may be possible.

Option B — International Gateway (No KSA Company Required)

If neither Salla nor Zid can be used without KSA registration, the team will explore international payment providers that accept foreign-registered businesses and support payments in Saudi Arabia. These do not require a local Saudi entity to onboard:

  • Stripe — global API-first gateway, supports SAR, Mada, Apple Pay; accepts international merchant registration
  • Tap Payments — Gulf-region gateway, developer-friendly, supports Mada/STC Pay/Apple Pay, can onboard without a KSA entity in some cases
  • PayTabs — MENA-focused, supports foreign companies, covers all major KSA payment methods
  • 2Checkout / Verifone — international processor, no KSA registration required, supports SAR billing
Next Action: Confirm with Salla and Zid business teams whether a third-party SaaS platform can use their checkout as a hosted payment page with webhook callback. If confirmed, proceed with Salla/Zid. If not feasible, proceed with an international provider (Tap Payments or Stripe recommended) that does not require a Saudi company registration.

3.7 Refund Policy

No Refunds Policy: All subscription payments — for both teachers and schools — are non-refundable. Once a payment is made, the user retains full access to their subscription for the entire paid period, but no refund will be issued under any circumstances until the subscription period has fully elapsed.
  • Users retain access to all paid features until the end of their subscription period
  • No partial refunds for early cancellation — cancellation stops renewal but does not return funds
  • This policy applies to all billing durations: monthly, 6-month, and annual plans
ZATCA Credit Notes: If a credit note is ever legally required (e.g. double billing error), it must be generated by admin with a negative amount referencing the original invoice UUID, and submitted to ZATCA in Phase 2 — this does not constitute a user-initiated refund.

3.9 PDPL Compliance Note

In addition to ZATCA, the Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL) of Saudi Arabia — نظام حماية البيانات الشخصية — applies to all payment data handled by Abjad:

  • Payment data must be stored in Saudi Arabia or countries with equivalent data protection
  • School and teacher financial data must not be shared with third parties without explicit consent
  • Users have the right to request deletion of payment history — subject to the 6-year ZATCA archiving override for invoice records
ZATCA vs PDPL: The mandatory 6-year ZATCA invoice archiving requirement legally overrides individual deletion requests for invoice records. Users can be informed of this limitation transparently.
