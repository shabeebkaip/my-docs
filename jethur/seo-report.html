Issue Findings
SEO Issues — 30 Findings
#1
Real Estate Boilerplate Live on /solution/grc/
Detail'What Makes Us Different' contains copy about 'overlooked residential and commercial real estate' and 'neighbourhoods we invest in, manage, and develop.' Left-over template content on the core GRC product page. A quality rater or enterprise buyer seeing this would immediately distrust the site.
ImpactTrust destruction — enterprise buyers evaluating GRC vendors will exit immediately.
#2
JavaScript-Animated Statistics Show '0%' to All Crawlers and AI
Detail'Proven Results' section uses animated JS counters. Googlebot, Bing, and all AI retrieval systems read: 'Faster Risk Identification: 0%', 'Faster audit cycles: 0%', 'Faster compliance closure: 0%'. The strongest proof points are currently invisible — or worse, counterproductive.
ImpactSEO + AI visibility — search engines and LLMs see zero or negative social proof.
#3
No CDN Edge Caching — Every Request Hits Origin
DetailDespite Cloudflare, all pages return cache-control: max-age=0 and cf-cache-status: DYNAMIC. Cloudflare is acting as a proxy only, not a CDN. For a global Enterprise SaaS, this means full origin round-trip latency for every user worldwide.
ImpactPerformance — LCP, TTFB, and global page load times all suffer.
#4
Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions Point to href='#'
DetailBoth footer links are placeholder anchors. Enterprise procurement and legal teams will immediately notice this. It also creates GDPR/PDPA compliance exposure — particularly damaging for a GRC company that sells compliance solutions.
ImpactTrust + Legal — procurement teams and regulators check these links.
#5
Missing HSTS Header
DetailHTTPS is enforced by 301 redirect only. Without HSTS the browser never locks in HTTPS-only at the browser level. First-time visitors and bookmarked links may briefly hit HTTP before redirect.
ImpactSecurity — browser-level HTTPS enforcement for all visitors.
#6
H1 Is Not the Actual Headline
Detail'One Platform, Complete GRC Control' — the visible hero tagline — is inside <p> tags. The actual <h1> is a 36-word run-on sentence listing every product category. Search engines use H1 as the strongest on-page relevance signal.
#7
About Page Is Thin and Anonymous (~350 words)
DetailNo founding year. No employee count. No leadership names. No team bios. Competitors like Hyperproof and Vanta feature named leadership with credentials. Google's quality raters specifically check About pages for trust signals.
ImpactE-E-A-T — About page is where enterprise buyers evaluate credibility.
#8
Zero Named Authors — All Blog Content by 'jethurmarketing'
DetailEvery blog post shows a WordPress username, not a human expert. For compliance content targeting CISOs and risk managers, author credibility is critical. Google's Helpful Content system evaluates whether content demonstrates first-hand expertise.
ImpactE-E-A-T — author expertise is a core ranking signal for YMYL topics like compliance.
#9
Blog Posts 850–950 Words — Min 1,500 for Competitive GRC Keywords
DetailPosts fall ~40% short of the competitive minimum. Top-ranking GRC content from Sprinto, Vanta, and Drata averages 2,000–3,000 words with original examples, data points, and framework-specific guidance.
#10
Mobile 'Schedule Demo' CTA Hidden on Tablet and Mobile
DetailThe nav CTA has elementor-hidden-tablet and elementor-hidden-mobile classes. On the highest-traffic device segments for awareness-stage enterprise buyers, there is no persistent CTA. This directly suppresses conversion.
ImpactConversion — mobile visitors have no path to demo request.
#11
Blog Pagination Canonical Is Broken
Detail/blog/page/2/ has a canonical pointing to /blog/ (page 1). This tells Google all paginated pages are duplicates of the index, suppressing crawl of older blog content.
ImpactIndexing — older blog posts may never be discovered by Google.
#12
/solution/ and /solutions/ Both in Sitemap — One Is a 301 Redirect
DetailTwo URL patterns coexist: /solution/grc/ and /solutions/ hub. The /solution/ parent redirects, but child /solution/grc/ is live — creating structural confusion for crawlers.
#13
CMS Versions Exposed in Meta Tags
DetailWordPress 6.8.1, Elementor 3.29.2, and Site Kit 1.167.0 are all visible in generator meta tags. This discloses the exact attack surface to potential attackers.
#14
jQuery Migrate Loaded on Production
DetailAdds ~10KB of unnecessary parse weight. jQuery Migrate is a backward-compatibility shim that is rarely needed in production.
#15
If-So Conditional Content Plugin — Cloaking Risk
DetailThe If-So plugin serves different content based on visitor signals (geo, device, referrer). If Googlebot sees different content than regular visitors, this constitutes cloaking — a manual action risk.
ImpactPenalty risk — cloaking can trigger a Google manual action.
#16
Article Schema Misapplied on Every Non-Blog Page
DetailHomepage, About, Products, Contact, Blog index are all incorrectly typed as Article by Rank Math's default. Sends incorrect semantic signals to search engines about every key page.
#17
Zero SoftwareApplication Schema — Biggest Missed Rich Result for a SaaS
DetailThe /products/ and /solution/grc/ pages have no SoftwareApplication schema. This is the most valuable structured data type for a SaaS platform — enables rich results showing category, features, pricing, and operating system.
ImpactRich results — SoftwareApplication schema directly enables enhanced SERP listings.
#18
Organization.sameAs Only References the Site Itself
DetailThe Organization schema's sameAs property links back to jethur.com instead of external profiles. sameAs is the primary mechanism for Google Knowledge Panel eligibility.
#19
~25 Out of 30 Images Have Empty alt=''
DetailAffects: hero product screenshot, site logo, all certification badges, all feature icons, and all solution illustration SVGs. Empty alt text means search engines cannot understand image content and visually impaired users get no context.
#20
OG Images Are Wrong Dimensions and Format
DetailHomepage OG image is a square logo (1:1) which renders with black bars on all social platforms. Products OG image is a 256×256px checkbox icon — far too small and completely generic.
ImpactSocial sharing — LinkedIn and Slack previews are the primary B2B sharing channels.
#21
No Regional Keyword Intent Despite Saudi HQ
Detail'NCA ECC,' 'SAMA CSF,' 'PDPL,' and 'Saudi Vision 2030' are almost entirely absent from product and homepage copy. Jethur's strongest competitive differentiator is deep regional regulatory expertise — and it's not on the page.
#22
Product Naming Inconsistency: 'Jethur' vs 'RootX' vs 'Jethur GRC'
Detail/solution/grc/ page title says 'RootX Comprehensive GRC Platform.' Other pages use 'Jethur GRC,' 'Jethur Platform,' or simply 'Jethur.' This confuses search engines about the brand entity.
#23
Add Hero Image Preload Hint
DetailThe hero image (home-banner-1-1024x642.webp) loads without a preload hint, meaning the browser doesn't begin fetching it until CSS parsing is complete.
#24
Convert Nohemi Font to .woff2
DetailThe custom brand font (Nohemi) is only available in .woff format. .woff2 is ~30% smaller and supported by all modern browsers. Only .woff causes a Flash of Unstyled Text (FOUT) on headlines.
#25
Expand FAQ on /solution/grc/ to 8–10 Questions
DetailThe current 4-question FAQ is the only answer-first content on the site. FAQ content is the primary source for AI citation and Google AI Overviews.
ImpactAI citation readiness — FAQ content is the #1 source for LLM citations.
#26
Add Social Proof Above the Fold
DetailThe site has exactly 1 named client reference (DeluPay, on /solution/grc/). No client logos, testimonials, G2/Gartner ratings, or industry references appear above the fold on any page.
ImpactConversion + Trust — social proof is the #1 driver of enterprise buyer confidence.
#27
Disable WP REST API for Unauthenticated Users
Detail/wp-json/wp/v2/pages returns full page structure including titles, slugs, and content to any unauthenticated request. Exposes site architecture and content to competitors and scrapers.
#28
Surface Certifications and Awards on Product Pages
DetailISO certifications (security, AI governance, BCM, risk management) and RegTech100 2026 recognition appear only in the Newsroom. These are the highest-credibility E-E-A-T signals and are buried where they have the least impact.
#29
Audit Thin/Noindex Candidates in Sitemap
DetailSeveral URLs may be hurting crawl quality: /download-datasheet/ (if gated), /newsletter/ (likely form-only), /regional-precision/ (unclear purpose), and category archive pages without introductory descriptions.
#30
Enable Elementor Per-Page Asset Loading
DetailElementor's full JS bundle loads on every page, including pages that don't use Elementor widgets. Contact Form 7 / WPForms scripts also load on pages with no forms.
