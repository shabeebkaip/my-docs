01 · Overview

Jethur is a well-positioned GRC SaaS platform with strong regional expertise across KSA, MENA, APAC, and Europe, and meaningful credibility signals — most notably the RegTech100 2026 recognition. Despite the strength of the product, the current website significantly underperforms as a sales and marketing asset.

This audit documented 25 issues across 7 categories of the current site. The most critical of these are active conversion failures: the header disappears on scroll, the only call-to-action commits visitors to an immediate demo booking, there is no pricing information, no case studies, and no visual demonstration of the product anywhere. For enterprise buyers evaluating GRC software, these are disqualifying gaps.

Additional problems exist at the technical level — the homepage returns a 403 Forbidden error to direct HTTP requests, meaning search engine crawlers may not be able to index the site. Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions links in the footer are broken, pointing to "#". These are not UX issues — they are legal and credibility risks that active prospects will encounter.

03 · Detailed Findings
A Navigation & Header 3 issues Critical
F-01 Critical Header Is Not Sticky — Primary CTA Disappears on Scroll

The site header does not remain fixed as users scroll. On long pages — the homepage, product pages, and /jethur-ai — the "Schedule Demo" button vanishes entirely once the user scrolls past the fold. Enterprise buyers often spend several minutes reading content before acting; by that point the only CTA on the page is gone.

F-02 High No Active Page Indicator in Navigation

Navigation links have no visual state indicating the current page. Users navigating between product and solution pages have no orientation cue — the nav looks identical on every page, which reduces confidence and increases the chance of users losing their place.

F-03 Medium Navigation Does Not Surface Pricing or Case Studies

The current navigation structure has no direct link to a pricing page or a case studies/resources section — both of which enterprise buyers navigate to immediately. These pages don't exist on the current site, but their absence from navigation also means there is no expectation set that they might exist elsewhere.

B CTAs & Conversion 4 issues Critical
F-04 Critical Only One CTA Exists Across the Entire Site

Every CTA on the site — on every page — directs the visitor to "Schedule Demo". There is no alternative: no content download, no product overview, no pricing inquiry, no newsletter. Visitors who are not yet ready to commit to a meeting — which is the majority on a first visit — have no engagement option and leave without any contact being made.

F-05 High No CTAs Within Long-Form Page Content

Product pages, solution pages, and the Standards & Regulations section contain long-form content with no in-body CTAs. A user who reads to the bottom of the ISO 27001 page, for example, has demonstrated intent — but encounters no next step. The page simply ends.

F-06 Medium CTA Button Styling Is Inconsistent Across Pages

Primary CTA buttons vary in colour, size, and visual treatment between pages. There is no consistent component — the button on the homepage looks different from the one on /jethur-ai and different again on solution pages. This inconsistency signals a lack of systematic design and can undermine perceived professionalism for buyers scrutinising the site carefully.

F-07 Low No Re-engagement Mechanism for Exiting Visitors

There is no exit-intent mechanism, no "save for later" option, and no confirmed remarketing pixel active on the site. Visitors who leave — especially those who browsed multiple pages — are lost entirely with no path to re-engagement.

C Pricing & Transparency 3 issues Critical
F-08 Critical No Pricing Page Exists

There is no pricing page, pricing section, or any pricing signal anywhere on the current site. Enterprise GRC buyers consistently cite pricing transparency as a top evaluation factor — even a "contact us for pricing" page communicates that pricing exists and is obtainable. The complete absence forces buyers to commit to a sales conversation before they can assess whether Jethur is in their budget, which most will not do.

F-09 High No Pricing Model Communicated Anywhere

There is no indication of how Jethur is priced — not by module, by seat, by organisation size, or as an enterprise contract. This is distinct from having no pricing page: the pricing model itself is entirely invisible. Buyers cannot even begin to estimate commercial fit.

F-10 Medium No Self-Serve or Low-Friction Entry Point

Competing platforms including Vanta and LogicGate offer free trials, interactive product tours, or compliance readiness assessments as entry points that don't require scheduling. Jethur's only entry point is a calendar booking, which is the highest-friction option available and filters out a significant portion of evaluators at the awareness stage.

D Trust & Social Proof 4 issues Critical
F-11 Critical Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions Links Are Broken

Both the Privacy Policy and Terms & Conditions links in the footer point to "#" — they navigate nowhere. This is a legal risk under GDPR and PDPL, and a direct trust failure. Enterprise procurement and legal teams check these pages as a standard step in vendor evaluation. A GRC platform with broken legal links is immediately disqualifying for buyers in regulated industries.

F-12 Critical No Case Studies or Client Success Evidence

There are no case studies, client testimonials with outcomes, or success story pages on the current site. For enterprise GRC software — where decisions involve multiple stakeholders, long sales cycles, and meaningful compliance risk — peer validation is the single most influential trust signal. Competitors AuditBoard and MetricStream lead with detailed case studies. The current site has none.

F-13 High RegTech100 2026 Recognition Not Visible in Key Locations

Jethur's inclusion in the RegTech100 2026 list is a significant, independently verified credibility signal. It is not displayed in the hero section, the homepage trust area, or the navigation. It appears in a location that most visitors will not see. This is a high-value signal being wasted on the current site.

F-14 Medium No Client Logos or Partner Logos on Homepage

The homepage does not display any client logos, partner logos, or "trusted by" messaging. Even a single recognised logo from a client or integration partner would materially increase perceived legitimacy. The current homepage offers no social proof of this kind.

E Content & Product Showcase 4 issues Critical
F-15 Critical No Product Visual in Hero — Visitors Cannot See the Product

The homepage hero contains no screenshot, UI preview, demo video, or any visual representation of the Jethur product. Visitors arrive at the site and leave having seen no evidence of what the software looks like. For enterprise software buyers, this is a fundamental gap — "what does this look like in practice" is the first question, and the current site provides no answer.

F-16 High Product Pages Show No Product

The /products and /jethur-ai pages describe capabilities in text but contain no product screenshots, annotated UI illustrations, workflow diagrams, or feature walkthroughs. A buyer reading these pages has no visual context for what they are being asked to evaluate.

F-17 High AI Page Makes General Claims Without Specifics

The /jethur-ai page describes AI capabilities in broad, generic terms — what the AI does, what it takes as input, what it produces, and how it compares to manual processes are all absent. In a market saturated with broad AI claims, the current copy is indistinguishable from competitors and provides no competitive information.

F-18 Low Blog Is Sparse and Infrequently Updated

The blog has limited content and shows infrequent updates. For a GRC platform, a blog is both an SEO asset and a trust signal — it signals that the company has active expertise in the compliance space. The current blog conveys neither.

F Technical & SEO 4 issues Critical
F-19 Critical Homepage Returns 403 Forbidden to Direct HTTP Requests

jethur.com returns a 403 Forbidden error to direct HTTP requests. This was identified during this audit. The implication is that search engine crawlers — Googlebot, Bingbot — may be receiving the same 403 response, preventing the homepage from being indexed. A site that cannot be crawled cannot rank in search. This was confirmed by the inability to fetch the homepage directly; it was assessed via cached data and sub-page access.

F-20 High Site Indexability at Risk for Some Search Engines

As a consequence of the 403 issue above, the homepage's presence in search results is uncertain. Jethur has invested in 7+ compliance framework pages (ISO 27001, SOC 2, NCA ECC, etc.) that carry organic search value — if the site's crawlability is compromised, this content may not be reaching the compliance professionals searching for it.

F-21 High No Structured Data / Schema Markup on Any Page

No schema markup (JSON-LD or microdata) was detected on any page of the current site. Structured data helps search engines categorise and display content as rich results. For a SaaS product, the absence of schema on product pages and the blog means the site is leaving search visibility potential unused.

F-22 Medium Meta Descriptions Missing or Generic on Key Pages

Multiple primary pages — including /products and /jethur-ai — either have no meta description or use generic, non-descriptive text. Meta descriptions are the first copy a prospect reads in a search result. The current site is not using this space to communicate a value proposition.

G UX & Branding 3 issues Medium
F-23 Medium Homepage Hero Lacks Clear Visual Hierarchy

The homepage hero does not guide the eye in a clear order. The headline, sub-headline, and CTA are insufficiently differentiated in size and weight, making it difficult for a first-time visitor to identify what Jethur is, who it is for, and what to do — within the 5-second window that most homepage visitors give a site before forming a first impression.

F-24 Low Compliance Pages Are Isolated — No Links to Product

The 7 compliance framework pages (ISO 27001, SOC 2, NCA ECC, etc.) function as standalone content with no internal links to product pages. A visitor arriving via search on one of these pages — likely a high-intent compliance professional — reads the content and hits a dead end with no path toward the product.

F-25 Low Demo Request Form Collects Minimal Qualification Data

The current demo request and contact form collects minimal information, making it difficult to triage inbound leads by intent, industry, or compliance need. The form does not ask for organisation size, primary compliance framework, or region — information that would allow the sales team to prioritise and prepare for conversations.

04 · Audit Methodology

This audit was conducted in March 2026 using the following approach:

  • Direct page analysis of all publicly accessible pages: /about-us, /products, /solution/grc, /jethur-ai, /standards-regulations, /blog, /contact, /schedule-a-demo, /regional-precision, and all 7 compliance framework pages
  • Navigation structure, CTA placement, content quality, trust signals, and conversion flow review
  • Site structure, footer links, social channels, and certification display assessment
  • SEO assessment including crawlability testing — homepage returned 403 to direct fetch, documented as F-19
  • Competitive benchmarking against: Vanta, AuditBoard, MetricStream, LogicGate
Note: The homepage (jethur.com) returned a 403 Forbidden error during direct fetch. It was assessed using Google cache data, search result snippets, and information accessible via all sub-pages. This 403 behaviour is documented as F-19.
