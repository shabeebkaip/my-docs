01 — Goals & Purpose

What the website must achieve

The Bznx website is not a digital brochure. It is a commercial asset — built to attract organic search traffic, establish trust, and convert visitors into paying clients.

🎯

Drive Organic Search Traffic

Rank on Google for the terms that entrepreneurs, investors, and business owners in Saudi Arabia are actively searching — capturing high-intent visitors before they engage a competitor.

🏛️

Establish Credibility & Authority

Present Bznx as the most knowledgeable and trusted business setup firm in the Kingdom. First impressions must convey expertise, professionalism, and reliability.

📥

Generate Inbound Enquiries

Every page should move visitors toward a contact action — WhatsApp, phone call, or enquiry form. Lead generation is the primary commercial function of the site.

🌍

Serve a Bilingual Audience

Reach both Arabic-speaking local and regional audiences, and English-speaking international investors and entrepreneurs exploring Saudi Arabia as a business destination.

02 — Target Audience

Who we are building for

The website must speak directly to three distinct audiences — each with different intent, language preference, and decision-making context.

🇸🇦

Saudi & GCC Entrepreneurs

Individuals planning to launch a new business, expand an existing one, or navigate licensing and regulatory requirements in the Kingdom. Primary Arabic and English speakers.

🌐

International Investors & Businesses

Foreign companies and investors looking to enter Saudi Arabia — needing guidance on entity types, foreign ownership rules, free zones, and compliance. English-dominant audience.

🏢

Corporate Decision-Makers

Finance directors, legal teams, and executives at regional businesses needing corporate restructuring, PRO services, or compliance support. LinkedIn-acquired traffic.

📚

Research-Stage Visitors

People who are 3–12 months from starting a business and are researching requirements, costs, and options. These visitors need educational content to build trust before they convert.

URL Structure

Clean, keyword-rich URLs. Use lowercase with hyphens. Example: /company-formation-saudi-arabia — never use dynamic parameters in public-facing URLs.

Bilingual Structure

Two separate URL paths: /en/ for English and /ar/ for Arabic. Implement hreflang tags on every page so Google serves the correct language version to the right audience.

Internal Linking

Every service page must link to related pages and back to the homepage. The blog must link to relevant service pages using exact-match anchor text. This is the primary SEO authority mechanism — not optional.

Blog as SEO Engine

The blog is the most important long-term SEO asset. A minimum of 2 published articles at launch and a publishing cadence of 4–6 posts per month is required to build domain authority within 6–12 months.

05 — SEO Requirements

Built to rank from day one

SEO must be built into the website architecture and every page — not added as an afterthought. These requirements are non-negotiable for achieving organic visibility in Saudi search results.

🏷️

On-Page SEO

  • Unique, keyword-optimised title tag on every page (50–60 chars)
  • Unique meta description on every page (150–160 chars)
  • One H1 per page containing the primary keyword
  • H2/H3 hierarchy supporting secondary keywords
  • Image alt text on every image — descriptive, keyword-relevant
  • Internal linking between related pages with descriptive anchor text
  • Clean, short, keyword-rich URLs — no parameters
  • Keyword-rich first paragraph on every page
⚙️

Technical SEO

  • XML sitemap — auto-generated and submitted to Google Search Console
  • Robots.txt — configured correctly, blocking admin areas only
  • Canonical tags on all pages to prevent duplicate content
  • Structured data (Schema.org) — Organization, Service, FAQ, Article
  • HTTPS — SSL certificate installed and enforced
  • 301 redirects from old URLs if relaunching an existing site
  • No broken links (404 errors) at launch
  • Mobile-first indexing compatibility
  • Core Web Vitals passing thresholds (LCP, FID, CLS)
📍

Local SEO (Saudi Arabia)

  • Google Business Profile — created, verified, and fully completed
  • NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistent across site and all directories
  • LocalBusiness schema on Contact and Homepage
  • Embedded Google Map on Contact page
  • City-specific landing page if Bznx operates across multiple cities
  • Reviews strategy — encourage Google reviews post-service
  • Arabic content targeting Arabic-language search queries
🌐

Bilingual & International SEO

  • Hreflang tags on every page linking EN ↔ AR versions
  • Separate translated content — not Google Translate
  • Arabic URLs using transliterated slugs (e.g. /ar/tasis-sharka)
  • Right-to-left (RTL) layout for Arabic version
  • Arabic meta titles and descriptions
  • Arabic sitemap submitted separately
  • lang="ar" attribute on Arabic HTML documents
⚡

Page Speed & Performance

  • Target: PageSpeed score 90+ on mobile and desktop
  • Images compressed and served in WebP format
  • Lazy loading on images below the fold
  • CSS and JavaScript minified and deferred where possible
  • CDN implementation for static asset delivery
  • No render-blocking resources in head
  • Font loading optimised — preconnect to Google Fonts
  • LCP target: under 2.5 seconds on mobile
🔗

Off-Page & Link Building Foundation

  • Business listings on Saudi directories (Maroof, Yellow Pages KSA, etc.)
  • Social profile links pointing to the website (LinkedIn, Instagram, etc.)
  • Press release or announcement content ready at launch
  • Shareable resources (guides, checklists) to attract inbound links
  • Blog content formatted for sharing and citation
  • Google Business Profile link to website — verified

07 — Content Requirements

Content that ranks and converts

Every piece of content must serve a dual function: satisfy Google's quality and relevance criteria, and move a human visitor closer to making an enquiry.

Minimum 1,200 Words on Core Service Pages

Google favours comprehensive content on commercial pages. Each service page must fully explain the topic — not just list Bznx's offerings.

FAQ Section on Every Service Page

5–8 real questions clients ask, answered clearly. Enables FAQ schema markup and can win Google featured snippets — free position-zero traffic.

Bilingual — Human Translation Only

Arabic content must be written by a native Arabic speaker or professionally translated. Auto-translation is detectable by Google and will not rank. Arabic content needs its own keyword research.

Launch Blog — Minimum 4 Articles

The blog must not launch empty. At least 4 high-quality articles (800+ words each) should be published on day one to signal content depth to Google crawlers.

No Duplicate Content

Every page must have unique content. Do not copy-paste text between service pages or between language versions. Use canonical tags where necessary.

Social Proof & Trust Content

Client testimonials (with permission), number of clients served, years in operation, and team credentials must appear on the homepage and about page.

Up-to-Date Regulatory Information

Saudi regulations change frequently. All regulatory and procedural content must include a "Last Updated" date and be reviewed quarterly. Outdated content damages both trust and rankings.

Launch Blog Topics (Suggested)

1. "How to Start a Business in Saudi Arabia in 2026 — Complete Guide"

2. "LLC vs. Branch Office vs. Representative Office in KSA — What's the Difference?"

3. "MISA Licence Explained: Foreign Investment in Saudi Arabia"

4. "Complete Checklist: Documents Needed to Register a Company in Saudi Arabia"

Content Voice & Tone

Professional but approachable. Never bureaucratic. Write for the entrepreneur who is slightly overwhelmed and needs clear, confident guidance. Avoid jargon. Use plain language. The goal is to make complex regulatory processes feel manageable — and Bznx the obvious partner to navigate them.

Content Update Schedule

Regulatory and service pages: reviewed every quarter. Blog: minimum 4 new posts per month (coordinated with Aura Marketer social content). All content must reflect current MISA, CR, and Zakat requirements.

08 — Lead Generation & CTAs

Turning visitors into enquiries

The website must have a clear and consistent conversion strategy — making it effortless for any visitor, at any point in the site, to reach out to Bznx.

💬

WhatsApp Integration

  • Floating WhatsApp button — visible on every page, bottom-right
  • Pre-filled message: "Hi, I'd like to enquire about business setup in Saudi Arabia."
  • Separate WhatsApp links per service page with relevant pre-filled text
  • WhatsApp CTA at end of every blog post
  • Click tracked in Google Analytics as a conversion event
📋

Enquiry Forms

  • Full enquiry form on Contact page (name, phone, email, service interest, message)
  • Short inline form on service pages ("Get a Free Consultation")
  • Form submission triggers thank-you page (/thank-you) — tracked as GA goal
  • Auto-response email sent to user confirming receipt
  • CRM notification / email alert to Bznx team on every submission
  • Spam protection via reCAPTCHA or Cloudflare Turnstile
📍

Persistent CTAs Across Pages

  • Phone number in the navigation header — click-to-call on mobile
  • CTA button above the fold on every page ("Start Your Business")
  • Mid-page CTA block after 40–50% of content on service pages
  • Footer-level CTA section on every page ("Ready to start? Talk to us.")
  • Exit-intent popup (optional, phase 2) — offer a free guide download
📊

CTA Copy & Messaging

  • Primary CTA: "Get a Free Consultation" — no commitment, reduces friction
  • Secondary CTA: "WhatsApp Us Now" — instant, familiar channel in Saudi Arabia
  • Urgency-free messaging — no fake "limited time" pressure
  • Arabic CTAs must be culturally adapted — not literal translations
  • CTA buttons in Arabic should use WhatsApp as the primary action channel
09 — Technical Requirements

Built for performance and reliability

Technical quality directly impacts both search rankings and user trust. These requirements are the minimum standard for the Bznx website.

📱

Mobile-First Design

  • Fully responsive — all screen sizes from 320px upward
  • Touch-friendly tap targets (min. 44×44px)
  • Mobile navigation — hamburger or bottom menu
  • Click-to-call phone number on mobile
  • WhatsApp button accessible without scrolling on mobile
  • Font sizes legible without pinch-zoom
⚡

Performance Standards

  • PageSpeed Insights: 90+ mobile, 95+ desktop
  • LCP (Largest Contentful Paint): under 2.5s
  • CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift): under 0.1
  • FID / INP (Interaction to Next Paint): under 200ms
  • Time to First Byte (TTFB): under 600ms
  • Total page weight under 2MB (compressed)
🔒

Security

  • SSL/TLS certificate — HTTPS enforced across all URLs
  • HTTP to HTTPS redirects — 301 permanent
  • Security headers: CSP, HSTS, X-Frame-Options
  • Form spam protection (reCAPTCHA v3 or Turnstile)
  • Admin area protected — non-guessable URL and 2FA
  • Regular automated backups
🌍

Hosting & Infrastructure

  • Server located in or near Saudi Arabia / Middle East region
  • 99.9% uptime SLA minimum
  • CDN (Cloudflare or equivalent) for static assets
  • Staging environment for testing before publish
  • Daily automated backups with 30-day retention
  • Auto-renewal SSL certificate
♿

Accessibility

  • WCAG 2.1 AA compliance target
  • Colour contrast ratio 4.5:1 minimum for body text
  • All images have descriptive alt attributes
  • Form fields have associated labels
  • Keyboard navigation functional throughout
  • ARIA roles on interactive components
🛠️

CMS & Maintainability

  • Bznx team must be able to update blog and service pages without developer involvement
  • CMS: WordPress or equivalent — bilingual plugin (WPML or Polylang)
  • Blog publishing workflow with draft/review/publish states
  • Image compression handled automatically on upload
  • Handover with documentation and team training session
10 — Analytics & Tracking

Measure what matters

Without proper analytics, there is no way to know whether the website is working or where to improve. All tracking must be in place on launch day.

📈

Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

  • GA4 property created and linked to website
  • Conversion events: form submit, WhatsApp click, phone click
  • Thank-you page (/thank-you) set as goal destination
  • Language dimension tracking (EN vs AR sessions)
  • Audience segments per service page
  • Monthly traffic and conversion reports
🔍

Google Search Console

  • Property verified for both www and non-www versions
  • Sitemap submitted on launch day
  • Monitor keyword impressions and click-through rates
  • Track crawl errors and fix within 48 hours
  • Monitor Core Web Vitals performance report
  • Check for manual penalties monthly
🗺️

Google Tag Manager

  • GTM container installed — all tags managed through GTM
  • WhatsApp click event tag
  • Phone number click event tag
  • Form submission event tag
  • Scroll depth tracking on blog articles
  • Future ad tracking tags added via GTM only
🌡️

Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity

  • Session recordings — understand how visitors navigate
  • Heatmaps on homepage and service pages
  • Form analytics — identify abandonment points
  • Free with Microsoft Clarity — install at launch
📊

Key Metrics to Track

  • Organic sessions (from Google / Bing)
  • Keyword rankings — top 10 target keywords
  • Enquiry form submissions per month
  • WhatsApp click events per month
  • Bounce rate per page
  • Average session duration on service pages
  • Language breakdown (EN vs AR)
🎯

Reporting Cadence

  • Monthly SEO performance report: rankings, traffic, conversions
  • Quarterly content audit: identify underperforming pages
  • Quarterly technical audit: crawl errors, speed regressions
  • Weekly check: Search Console errors and new rankings
  • Dashboard shared with Bznx team (Google Looker Studio)
11 — Delivery Timeline

From brief to live in 4 weeks

A structured 4-week delivery plan ensures the website is built correctly, reviewed thoroughly, and launched with all SEO foundations in place.

Week 1

Discovery, Strategy & Content Collection

Kickoff meeting, brand asset collection (logo, colours, fonts), content brief to Bznx team, keyword confirmation, wireframe sitemap approval, and brief sign-off. Bznx to provide: service descriptions, team bios, client testimonials, and company photos.

Week 2

Design — Mockups & Approval

Homepage design mockup (desktop + mobile) presented for review. Design system established: typography, colours, button styles, component library. Up to 2 rounds of design revisions. Final design approval before development begins.

Week 3

Development & Content Population

Full site development on staging environment: all pages built, bilingual structure implemented, WhatsApp integration, forms, SEO meta tags, schema markup, Analytics + GTM setup. Blog articles drafted and entered into CMS.

Week 4

QA, Testing & Launch

Full cross-browser and device testing, PageSpeed optimisation, broken link check, form testing, hreflang validation, sitemap submission to Google Search Console, DNS cutover, SSL verification, and post-launch monitoring. Team training on CMS.

12 — Design Direction

A brand that commands confidence

The design must project authority, professionalism, and trustworthiness — the qualities a client needs to feel before handing over a business formation engagement.

🎨

Visual Identity

Use Bznx brand colours and logo consistently. Typography should combine a serif font for headings (authority) with a clean sans-serif for body (readability). Dark backgrounds for hero sections convey premium positioning.

📐

Layout & Hierarchy

Clear visual hierarchy — H1 headlines dominate, CTAs stand out. Generous whitespace. Grid-based layout. Content scannable by eye. Avoid cluttered design — less is more for a premium professional service.

🌙

Arabic (RTL) Version

Full RTL layout for Arabic pages — not just text alignment, but mirrored icon positions, navigation direction, and component layout. Arabic typography must use an appropriate Arabic web font (e.g. Cairo, Tajawal, or IBM Plex Arabic).

📸

Photography & Visual Assets

Real photos of the team and office are strongly preferred over generic stock imagery. If stock is used, choose images that reflect the Saudi / GCC business context. All images WebP format, compressed to under 150KB per image.
