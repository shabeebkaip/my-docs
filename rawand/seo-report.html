Executive Summary
What We Found

Rawand Trading Company operates a functional Zid-based e-commerce store at rawand.com.sa with 350+ products across 26 categories in a bilingual EN/AR format. The site has a solid structural foundation — CDN delivery, lazy-loaded images, tracking pixels (Meta, TikTok, Snapchat, GTM), and LocalBusiness schema. However, a cluster of critical technical SEO failures means Google cannot correctly identify, classify, or rank the site's bilingual content.

The most damaging issues are the complete absence of hreflang tags and canonical tags across all pages — causing Google to treat the EN and AR versions as duplicate competing content. Combined with missing meta descriptions, a non-existent English H1 on the homepage, and zero content marketing infrastructure, the site is leaving significant organic traffic and revenue on the table.

Critical Issues (3)

3 Issues
C-01
Missing Hreflang Tags — EN and AR Versions Competing Against Each Other
Finding
No <link rel="alternate" hreflang="..."> tags exist on any page. The site serves /en-sa/ and /ar-sa/ versions with no signals to Google about their relationship. Google treats both as independent, competing pages — potentially showing the Arabic version to English searchers and vice versa.
Impact
Bilingual ranking is undefined. Both language versions may compete for the same queries, splitting link equity and causing ranking instability. Google may suppress one version entirely as a duplicate.
Fix
Add to every page's <head>: <link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-SA" href="https://rawand.com.sa/en-sa/[page]" />, <link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-SA" href="https://rawand.com.sa/ar-sa/[page]" />, <link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://rawand.com.sa/" />. Configure in Zid theme settings or request via Zid support.
C-02
No Canonical Tags Anywhere — Duplicate Content at Scale
Finding
Zero <link rel="canonical"> tags detected on homepage, category pages, product pages, or blog posts. With 350+ products, filter combinations, and bilingual duplicates, Google is encountering hundreds of duplicate URL variants with no canonical signal.
Impact
Link equity diluted across duplicate URLs. Crawl budget wasted on parameterized URLs. Google may index the wrong URL variant for key pages, preventing proper ranking.
Fix
Every page must self-reference with a canonical: <link rel="canonical" href="https://rawand.com.sa/en-sa/[exact-page-url]" />. EN and AR pages should each canonicalize to themselves (not cross-language). Check Zid's SEO settings panel — this may be configurable without custom code.
C-03
Meta Descriptions Missing on All Product and Category Pages
Finding
Product pages have no <meta name="description"> tag. The company description appears only inside JSON-LD schema (not picked up by search engines for snippets). On homepage, a brand tagline is used — not a search-intent-driven snippet. Blog articles have no meta description.
Impact
Google writes its own snippets from page content — typically low-quality, non-persuasive. CTR loss estimated at 20–30% vs. optimized meta descriptions. This affects all 350+ product pages immediately.
Fix
Write unique 140–160 char meta descriptions for: (1) Homepage EN: "Shop HP, Canon & Xerox printers, copiers, toners and office furniture in Riyadh. Order online — same-day delivery across Saudi Arabia." (2) Each category: include brand names + "buy in Saudi Arabia / Riyadh". (3) Each product: model + price + "free delivery Riyadh". Implement via Zid product/category SEO fields.

High Priority Issues (8)
High Priority — Content & Discoverability

8 Issues
H-01
No H1 Tag on English Homepage — Multiple H1s on Arabic Homepage
Finding
English homepage has zero H1 tags — the most important on-page signal. Arabic homepage has 6+ H1 tags (section headings like "Shop all products", "Offers and discounts" incorrectly tagged as H1). There should be exactly one H1 per page.
Fix
EN Homepage H1: "Office Printers, Copiers & Supplies | Riyadh, Saudi Arabia". AR Homepage H1: "طابعات وآلات تصوير ومستلزمات مكتبية في الرياض". Demote all section headings to H2.
H-02
Title Tags Generic, Duplicate, and Missing Local Keywords
Finding
Homepage EN title: "Rawand Trading Company for office supplies and electronics" — no location, no brand names, no differentiation. About Us page has an identical title to the homepage. Titles appear to be JS-rendered.
Fix
Homepage: "Office Printers, Copiers & Scanners | Rawand — Riyadh, KSA". Category pages: "[Category Name] — Buy Online in Saudi Arabia | Rawand". Products: "[Brand] [Model] | [Price] SAR — Rawand Riyadh". Ensure titles are in static HTML, not JS-injected.
H-03
Open Graph and Twitter Card Tags Completely Missing
Finding
No og:title, og:description, og:image, or Twitter Card tags on any page. Sharing any rawand.com.sa URL on WhatsApp, LinkedIn, Instagram, or X shows no image, no title, and no description — just a bare URL.
Impact
Every product shared on social media looks broken. For a brand running social campaigns, this silently kills click-through rates from organic shares.
Fix
Add to every page: og:title, og:description (150 chars), og:image (1200×630px), og:url, og:locale (en_SA / ar_SA). Add Twitter Card meta tags. Implement globally in Zid theme header. Create a default OG image with brand logo + "rawand.com.sa".
H-04
"Riyadh" and "Saudi Arabia" Absent From All Titles, H1s, and Meta Tags
Finding
The words "Riyadh", "Saudi Arabia", "KSA", "الرياض", or "المملكة" do not appear in any page title, H1, or meta description. For a location-specific B2B business, this is a critical local SEO failure.
Fix
Add "Riyadh" or "Saudi Arabia" to homepage title, H1, meta description, and homepage body copy. Add "الرياض" to all Arabic equivalents. Add to Google Business Profile. Ensures ranking for "printer supplier Riyadh" and similar local queries.
H-05
Homepage Has Zero Body Copy — Google Has Nothing to Index
Finding
The EN homepage is a product grid with section headings only. No introductory paragraph, no business description, no USPs in text form. Google's ranking algorithm needs readable prose content to understand what the page is about and who it should serve.
Fix
Add 150–250 words of keyword-rich copy to both EN and AR homepages: business introduction, brands carried, location ("serving Riyadh since..."), services, target customers. Place above or alongside the product grid. This alone can significantly improve homepage ranking for category-level queries.
H-06
All Product Pages Lack Unique Descriptions — Treated as Duplicate Content
Finding
Product descriptions appear to be manufacturer spec sheets — identical across all Saudi retailers carrying the same products. Google treats these as duplicate content and suppresses ranking.
Fix
Add 80–150 words of unique copy per product: use case, compatibility details, Saudi-specific delivery info, comparison note vs. similar model. Start with top 50 best-sellers. Batch-write using a template: "[Brand] [Model] is ideal for [use case] in [Saudi office context]. Compatible with [machines]. Available for next-day delivery in Riyadh..."
H-07
NAP Inconsistency — Two Different Addresses Listed
Finding
LocalBusiness schema shows address: "Al-Haware". About Us page shows: "Al-Malaz". These are inconsistent. Google and other search engines use NAP (Name, Address, Phone) consistency as a local trust signal.
Fix
Choose one correct address and use it identically across: website schema, About page, Google Business Profile, all directory listings. Format: "[Street], Al-[District], Riyadh 1XXXX, Saudi Arabia". Update Zid LocalBusiness schema to match.
H-08
No Blog / Content Hub — Zero Informational Search Capture
Finding
The site has ~5 English blog posts (2023 dates, 600–700 words, no schema, no author). Searches like "best office printer Saudi Arabia 2025", "HP vs Canon printer comparison", "printer maintenance Riyadh" each have hundreds of monthly searches — all going to competitors.
Fix
Launch a structured bilingual blog (Arabic primary, English secondary). Publish 2 articles per week. Priority topics: buyer's guides (HP vs Canon, laser vs inkjet), maintenance tips, product comparisons, local guides ("best copier for Riyadh law firms"). Target: 30 articles in 90 days.

Medium Priority Issues (7)
Medium Priority — Schema, Technical, & Local
Important for long-term authority and AI search visibility
7 Issues
M-01
Product Schema Missing on All 350+ Product Pages
Finding
LocalBusiness schema exists on homepage but Product schema (with price, availability, brand, SKU) is absent from product pages. This blocks Google Shopping rich results, star ratings, and price snippets in SERPs.
Fix
Add Product schema to all product pages with: name, brand, sku, offers (price in SAR, availability, priceCurrency), description. Also add AggregateRating once reviews are collected. Zid may support this natively — check platform settings.
M-02
robots.txt Blocks /js/ Directory — Prevents Googlebot from Rendering Pages
Finding
Disallow: /js/ in robots.txt prevents Googlebot from loading JavaScript files needed to render the page. Since metadata (title, meta description) appears JS-dependent, Googlebot may be indexing pages without their most important SEO elements.
Fix
Remove the Disallow: /js/ rule. Add Sitemap: https://rawand.com.sa/sitemap.xml directive to robots.txt. Verify rendering by using Google Search Console URL Inspection "Test Live URL" on homepage.
M-03
Category Pages Have No Introductory Copy
Finding
All 26 category pages are pure product grids with minimal text. Category-level keywords ("HP printers for offices", "Canon copier machines Saudi Arabia") cannot rank without supporting copy.
Fix
Add 100–200 words of category description to each page. Include: what the category covers, top brands available, use cases, why choose Rawand. Add an FAQ section at the bottom of each category page with FAQPage schema (5–8 Q&As).
M-04
No FAQPage, BlogPosting, or BreadcrumbList Schema
Finding
The scanner blog article has an FAQ section but no FAQPage schema. Blog articles lack BlogPosting schema (author, datePublished, dateModified). No breadcrumb navigation or BreadcrumbList schema on any page.
Fix
Add FAQPage schema to all category and product pages. Add BlogPosting schema to all blog articles (with author bio, ISO date format, word count). Add BreadcrumbList schema to all category/product pages. These enable rich results in Google SERPs.
M-05
Inconsistent URL Prefixes (/en-sa/ vs /en/) Across Page Types
Finding
Homepage uses /en-sa/ but category pages use /en/categories/. This prefix inconsistency may cause hreflang mapping errors once implemented and can confuse crawlers about the correct canonical locale.
Fix
Standardize all pages to a single prefix scheme. Recommended: /en-sa/ for all English content and /ar-sa/ for all Arabic content. If Zid platform doesn't support changing category URL patterns, document this inconsistency for the hreflang implementation.
M-06
AI Crawler Bots (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot) Likely Blocked
Finding
The robots.txt has an extensive 500+ bot blocklist. AI crawler bots (GPTBot for ChatGPT, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot, anthropic-ai) may be among those blocked. This prevents Rawand from appearing in AI-generated answers — a rapidly growing traffic source.
Fix
Explicitly add Allow rules for: User-agent: GPTBot / Allow: /, User-agent: PerplexityBot / Allow: /, User-agent: ClaudeBot / Allow: /, User-agent: anthropic-ai / Allow: /. Also add llms.txt at site root with plain-text business description for AI context.
M-07
Blog Content Outdated, Thin, and Under-Linked
Finding
Existing blog articles reference "2023" in titles — now 3 years old, damaging CTR. Articles are 600–700 words (need 1,500–2,500 for competitive queries). Blog section is not prominently linked from homepage or navigation. Policy pages share the same /blogs/ URL structure as editorial content.
Fix
Update all article titles to remove 2023 / add 2026. Expand each article to 1,500+ words. Add "Latest Articles" section to homepage with 3 featured posts. Separate policy pages to /pages/ URL structure. Add BlogPosting schema with current dateModified.
