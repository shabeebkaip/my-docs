The website also features customer reviews on the homepage, all 5 major social platforms, and a strong Eid Al-Adha promotional campaign — all stronger than initially estimated.

Homepage — rawand.com.sa/en-sa/
65/100
Strong visual identity: Red/white brand is bold, consistent, and professional. Eid Al-Adha seasonal banner is relevant and well-designed — shows active brand management.
Clear navigation: All Products, Maintenance Services, Offers & Discounts, Office Equipment, Inks, Office Furniture — logically structured for the buyer's journey.
Customer reviews section: Homepage testimonials carousel with 3–5 star Arabic reviews from real customers — excellent social proof. Includes جمعية عمارة بيوت الله (a housing association) confirming B2B buyers.
Delivery banner: "خدمة توصيل سريعة — شحن لجميع أنحاء المملكة" prominently shown with branded delivery vehicle imagery — builds purchase confidence.
"Why choose Rawand" section: Text copy addressing "Wide range of printers and inks" and "Guaranteed quality at competitive prices" — good for SEO and conversion.
Hero banner text not indexed: The large Arabic Eid text is embedded in an image. Search engines cannot read it. There is no visible H1 tag or text-based headline above the fold.
Category icons too small: The 5 category icons (Scanner, Printers, Copier Machines, Plotter, Inks) are compact tiles. On mobile, these may be hard to tap accurately and don't communicate product value visually.
No searchable keyword copy: "Riyadh", "Saudi Arabia", and brand names (HP, Canon, Xerox) do not appear in any indexable text above the fold — only in product cards below.
Product Pages — 350+ Products
50/100
Pricing clear with discounts: Original price crossed out with discounted price shown — standard e-commerce best practice. Discount percentages (4.56%, 11.34%) displayed as badges.
Add to cart button: Prominent, red CTA button consistent with brand. "Out of stock — notify me" functionality observed.
Product badges: "Special Product", "Newly arrived", "Selling fast", "Free shipping" — good urgency and trust signals.
No cross-sell / related products: A customer buying HP toner is not shown the compatible HP printer. A customer buying a printer is not prompted to add toner. This is leaving significant average order value on the table.
Thin product descriptions: Manufacturer spec sheets only. Google cannot differentiate Rawand's product pages from Jarir's or Amazon's for the same product. No unique copy.
No product reviews on individual pages: While the homepage shows testimonials, individual product pages show "لا يوجد تقيمات لهذا المنتج" (no reviews). Google Shopping ranking is affected.
Some products out of stock: Out-of-stock products remain visible and rank-eligible but cannot convert. Consider removing from navigation or adding "back in stock" prioritization.
Maintenance Services Page — rawand.com.sa/en/categories/1518604/
30/100
Category description exists: "Professional maintenance of copiers and printers, where a specialized technician is shown inspecting and repairing..." — good start, but needs expansion.
Only 1 product listed at SAR 230: The maintenance service category — which is one of the highest-value B2B offerings — is represented by a single generic listing. This is Rawand's biggest differentiator and it's completely undersold.
No service tiers or packages: No annual contract, no per-visit pricing, no brand-specific maintenance (HP maintenance vs Xerox maintenance), no response time SLA shown.
Not rankable: Single product page cannot rank for "printer repair Riyadh" or "printer maintenance service Saudi Arabia" without supporting content, FAQ, and multiple service listings.
No contact form or quote request: Enterprise buyers wanting maintenance contracts need a dedicated inquiry form. "Add to cart" is not the right CTA for a maintenance contract — it should be "Request a Quote" or "Book a Technician".

🔴 Why Performance Score 32–37 Is Costing Sales
Google uses Core Web Vitals as a ranking signal. A failed CWV assessment means Google actively pushes Rawand's pages down in organic results — even if the content is perfect.
Every extra second of load time costs ~7% conversion rate. At 3.2s LCP on mobile, a significant portion of Saudi visitors (80%+ on mobile) abandon before the page fully loads.
Root Causes & Fixes
🔴
TTFB 2.1s (Mobile) / 1.9s (Desktop) — Server Response Too Slow
The server is taking 2+ seconds just to respond before any content loads. This is a Zid platform/hosting issue. Fix: Enable Zid's CDN/edge caching, contact Zid support to optimize server response time. Target: under 0.8s.
LCP 3.2s — Hero Image (Eid Banner) Not Optimized
The large Eid Al-Adha hero banner is the Largest Contentful Paint element — it loads too slowly. Fix: Compress hero images to WebP format, add loading="eager" + fetchpriority="high" to the hero image, use Zid's image CDN. Target: under 2.5s.
Best Practices 50 — Likely JavaScript & Third-Party Script Issues
Score of 50 typically indicates issues with third-party scripts (pixels, chat widgets), deprecated APIs, or insecure resource loading. Fix: Audit GTM tags, defer non-critical pixels, remove unused scripts. The Facebook, TikTok, Snapchat pixels should load asynchronously.
Accessibility 75 — Missing Labels, Contrast Issues
Score of 75 indicates missing ARIA labels, insufficient color contrast, or images without alt text. Fix: Add alt text to all product images, ensure button labels are descriptive, check color contrast on red CTAs against white backgrounds.

Growth Opportunities
Highest-Impact Improvements
Urgent
Launch Google Shopping Campaigns
Rawand shows in text ads but NOT in Google Shopping product panels (where Jarir, Amazon, HP Store appear with product images and SAR prices). Google Shopping drives 85% of Google Ads clicks for e-commerce in 2025. Set up Google Merchant Center and submit all 350 products immediately.
Urgent
WhatsApp Business API Automation
WhatsApp is already the most-used contact method on the site. Upgrading to WhatsApp Business API enables: automated quote responses, reorder reminders for ink/toner buyers (every 60–90 days), cart abandonment recovery, and broadcast promotions. 95% open rate vs 2–5% for email.
Urgent
Expand Maintenance Services Page
Currently 1 product at SAR 230. Should be: Annual maintenance contracts (SAR X,XXX/year), per-visit emergency service, brand-specific (HP, Canon, Xerox, Sharp) maintenance packages, multi-machine fleet contracts. Add a "Request a Quote" form. This is Rawand's highest-margin, lowest-competition offering.
Urgent
Add Cross-Sell to All Product Pages
Printer buyers should see compatible toner. Toner buyers should see compatible printers. Copier buyers should see maintenance service. This single change can increase average order value by 20–35%. Zid supports "related products" functionality — configure it for the top 50 SKUs first.
High
Product Review Collection Campaign
Homepage testimonials are great but product pages show zero reviews. WhatsApp all past customers asking for product reviews. Offer a small incentive (5% off next order). Target 100 product reviews in 30 days. This improves Google Shopping ranking, conversion rate, and trust.
High
Open Graph Tags for Social Sharing
When a Rawand product is shared on WhatsApp or Instagram, it shows a blank URL with no image or description. Adding OG meta tags means every shared link shows a product image, name, and price — turning every share into a branded ad. Free to implement, takes 1 day.
High
Activate Snapchat & TikTok Ads
Snapchat (90.7% Saudi penetration) and TikTok (34M+ Saudi users) pixels are installed but no campaigns are running. This is ad spend left on the floor. Awareness campaigns targeting procurement managers and office administrators can fill the top of funnel at low CPM (SAR 10–25).
High
LinkedIn B2B Lead Generation
No LinkedIn presence found for Rawand. 7.1 million Saudi LinkedIn users. Create company page + run Lead Gen Form campaigns targeting Office Managers, Procurement Officers, IT Managers at 50–500 employee companies in Riyadh. "Request a quote for your office fleet" — direct B2B pipeline.
Medium
Ink/Toner Subscription Model
Ink and toner are consumables with predictable reorder cycles (60–90 days). Launch a "Rawand Subscription" — monthly toner delivery for offices with 5+ printers. Reduces customer effort, guarantees Rawand recurring revenue, and creates extreme switching costs. HP does this globally with HP Instant Ink.
Medium
Seasonal Campaign Infrastructure
The Eid Al-Adha banner shows active seasonal marketing. This should be systematized: Ramadan (March/April), Saudi National Day (September), Government fiscal year-end (December), Back to office (September), New Year procurement. Automated email + WhatsApp sequences for each window.
Summary
Overall Website Assessment

Rawand Trading Company has built a significantly stronger digital foundation than most Saudi B2B office equipment suppliers. The website is live, bilingual, tracking-enabled, paid-advertising-active, and offers a payment stack that rivals major retailers (including Tamara/Tabby BNPL). The brand identity is strong and consistent.

The gap is not in the foundation — it is in activation. Every channel pixel is installed but most channels have no campaigns. The maintenance service is Rawand's highest-value differentiator and it has essentially one product listed. Google Shopping is absent despite Google Search Ads running. WhatsApp has 95% open rates and Rawand uses it manually.

Expanding the maintenance service into a proper product line creates a recurring revenue stream that competitors cannot copy. The next phase is not about building — it is about turning on what is already there.
